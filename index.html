List 3 reasons why D3 was helpful and improved your visualization (6 points)

  1. D3 made it really easy for me to create a scale of colors to improve the aesthetics of my graph
  2. D3, using the scaleBand and scaleLinear functions allowed me to create scales for my graph axes without calculating manually what they should be
  3. D3 allowed me to easily create axis labels and transform them as I pleased — if I had manually drawn them, I would have to recalculate them if my data changed

List 3 reasons why D3 would not be the best tool for creating a visualization (6 points)

  1. D3 could be difficult for non-technical people to understand and thus pose a high barrier of entry
  2. D3 can only be used in JavaScript, so cannot be easily transferred to other mediums like print
  3. D3 is not as customizable as programs like Adobe Illustrator, where every possible attribute can be changed exactly as you please very easily
  4. D3 doesn't always support older browsers

Evaluate the accessibility of your dashboard based on the readings in the “Before you begin” section. What kinds of users might find this dashboard accessible and who might have more difficulty? What additional actions might you take to make this dashboard more accessible to all audiences? Your response should refer to at least one of the readings and be about 1 paragraph.

My dashboard accessibility is quite horrendous. First, there are no tooltips. Second, the text on some of the axes are quite small. However, things are well segmented in to structural elements, which makes it easy to navigate for screen readers. There are also no audio components, which make it very accessible to the deaf. Additionally, there is a small animation which may be distracting to those with ADHD (as mentioned in the article "Why Accessibility Is at the Heart of Data Visualization") I could add tool tips and remove animations, as well as making the fonts larger.
